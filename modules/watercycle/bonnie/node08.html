Finale

Bonnie and her students spent 14 days exploring the water cycle. Bonnie took copious notes, rewarded students for participation in the unit, examined their journals, and gave a final general writing assignment. In the assignment students were to discuss the question that launched the exploration, i.e., what happened to the puddle, and to write on what was memorable about the unit. Reflection. What ideas are expressed in the students' work? What are the strengths and weaknesses of giving this kind of assignment? What might be some strategies for assessing learning in a responsive learning environment?

The water cycle exploration occurred early in the school year. We did not prescribe target core ideas for the students to develop. Our goal was to study the process of teachers becoming responsive, and students' reactions to that. Despite not having specific target ideas, students engaged in scientific practices to develop ideas about the water cycle, as evidenced on the preceding pages. In future work, we plan to extend our goals to be responsive toward the NGSS.
