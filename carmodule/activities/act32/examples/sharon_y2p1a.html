Summary for Days 7 & 8

Continuing with Jamir's presentation of his ramp idea to the class, students discussed whether the first hill needed to be taller than the second hill for the car to make it over the second hill. In giving an analogy, Jamir shifted the conversation to a person jumping and falling off a diving board. The students then began debating about whether heavier or lighter objects fall faster or go down a ramp faster. Students tested their ideas by dropping lighter and heavier books, but the results were inconclusive. Then they did experiments with heavier and lighter cars going down a ramp.

 

Day 7

Students continue their conversation about Jamir's ramp. Jamir defends his idea; he says that if the first hill is taller than the second hill, the car will make it over the second hill. Alexis says that it might be able to make it over because the car is heavy, which makes it speedy. Scarlett disagrees with Jamir. She thinks that the car will go up the second hill, but then roll back on the same side. Maribel thinks that the second hill would need to be smaller in order for the car to make it. Sharon shifts the conversation back to Jamir's idea and puts what he told her earlier on the board: "when it's falling, it's kind of pushing." She asks what he means by that, and he says that the air is pushing it down, like when you jump off of a diving board and you feel the air pushing you.

The class has different ideas about heavier and lighter objects falling. Sharon puts both ideas on the board, drawing a vertical line between them. She asks student to think about which idea they think is right, and then to get up and sit on that side of the board.


Sharon has written the two competing ideas on the front board:  “Heavy things fall slower because
the wind can’t push the car” on the left, and “Heavy things fall faster” on the right.

After students are settled in two groups, Sharon notices Jennifer is crawling from the ‘heavy things fall slower’ side to the ‘heavy things fall faster’ side, and Sharon asks her why. This leads to an experiment that involves dropping a lighter and heavier book to see which one falls fastest. Different students drop the two books. Although it seems both landed at (about) the same time, the class does not agree on the observations. Teresa asks which side has the most students, but Sharon points out that what students think and predict isn’t necessarily what happens. She suggests they continue this discussion next time.

Day 8

Day 8 starts out with Sharon reminding the students of the two different positions that emerged at the end of the previous class: Priscilla's position is that a heavy thing will go faster, and Gustavo's position is that a lighter thing will go faster because the air can't push it as well. They tested it out with books, but weren't able to come to a definitive conclusion. She gives them time to try dropping books again or rolling cars down a ramp to see if the heavier or lighter cars/books will fall down first. She sits in on one of the groups and realizes that Jose and Brittany both disagree with each other. She asks them to try and reconcile their disagreement. They try dropping a heavier and lighter car down a ramp to see which one will get to the bottom first (Jose points out that once the cars hit the bottom, the test has to end).

Later, Sharon is having a conversation with Gustavo about why a lighter car will go faster than a heavier car, and he draws an analogy with a lighter person racing a heavier person. He thinks that the lighter person would be faster, but would get tired and run out of energy sooner. This leads to Sharon suggesting that she and Gustavo have a race at recess.

  • In explaining why he thinks a lighter car will go down the ramp faster than a heavier car, Gustavo draws an analogy with two people racing. He says the little (lighter) person would go faster, but would get tired more quickly, than the bigger (heavier) person. The bigger person would be able to go for a longer period of time because they have more energy. What seems good about Gustavo's idea is that he is both connecting it to prior experience (his racing a heavier friend) and justifying it with an energy-based reason. In that sense, his idea is clear, coherent, and causal.
