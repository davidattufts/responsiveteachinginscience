On Day 2, Carolyn asked the class how they might make the toy car go farther. In the video clip below, students discuss how the weight or the size of the car affected how far or how fast it would go.

Jamilla begins by stating that a small car will go further than a big car because it is lighter. Adrian doesn't believe that would be true if the cars were going down a hill (or a ramp) because, he says, gravity makes the heavier car go faster. Johnny gives a lengthy explanation for why he agrees with Adrian. Jamilla clarifies that her idea applied to a flat surface. On a ramp, though, she still argues that the smaller car would go faster and farther because it has less weight on it. So she still appears to disagree with Adrian. Later, Adrian says that if the smaller car is the heavier car, it will go further. Jamilla agrees with him.
