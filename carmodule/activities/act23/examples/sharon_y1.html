On Day 15, Sharon's class broke into small groups, and each group experimented with different types of cars to see if they could explain how to make the car moves, why it moves, and how energy gets to the car to make it move.

In the video clip below, Sharon is discussing the mechanics of propeller car motion with a group of four students: (from bottom left) Fabian, Caleb, Tracy, and Jewel. The students explain how the direction of the propeller spin determines the direction the car moves. After a demonstration by Fabian, Caleb indicates that the wind blows in a direction opposite the motion of the car. Sharon asks for a clarification: how can the wind be pushing in one direction, if the car is moving in another direction? The students attempt to explain the interaction between the fan (propellers), wind and car, and talk about their experiences with wind in their daily lives.
