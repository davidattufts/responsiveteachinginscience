On Day 9, students in Carolyn's class discussed what it takes to make a propeller car go. Johnnie thought that if you put the car outside on a windy day, the wind would spin the propeller and push it. But Adrian and Jamillah disagreed; they said that the car is too heavy, and the wind too light, for the wind to move the car.

During this discussion, Carolyn was winding up the rubber band on the propeller car by turning the propeller, which Hannah observed makes the rubber band "tighter" and, in the words of another student, "stronger." In a discussion about testing Johnnie's idea about the wind, Janet stated that the wind won't make the car go, but if the wind is behind the car, it could make it go a bit faster. Though she seems to agree with Adrian and Jamillah, Adrian explains why he actually thinks the wind would need to be in front of the car to make it go faster.
