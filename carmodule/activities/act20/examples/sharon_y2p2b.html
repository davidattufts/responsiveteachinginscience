Summary for Day 9

This is the last day students will be spending on toy cars for about five months. Sharon asks students to work with the solar car and the battery car, and figure out how they work.
 

Day 9

At the beginning of the period, Sharon introduces the task for the day. She wants students not only to make the cars (battery and solar) work, but also to dig deeper and figure out how they work.


Video Clip 1 (Day 9)

Most of the remaining video clips focus on one group trying to figure out how the battery car works. When working, the car moves and lights a bulb. Sharon interacts with the group during part of the time, and the group works independently the rest of the time. In Clip 2, the group calls over Sharon to show her what they have learned so far about connecting wires so the car moves and lights the bulb. Each time they show Sharon something, she asks them ‘why’ that happens.

  • The students (without Sharon present) are explaining to themselves both how the wires in the car are connected and how the battery is providing energy. This is a nice example of a group functioning well because they are intensely focused on the task at hand (figuring out how the car works) and add on to each others' comments. Their understanding of what is going on is co-constructed by the group during their interactions with the car and with each other.

     

While working with the car, Alexis discovers that one battery is very hot, while the other one is cold. They begin trying to understand why that happens.

  • After connecting the wires in a particular way, the students discover that one battery is very hot and the other is cold. They provide explanations for why this is. Video Clip 5 (Day 9)

Some things to notice: (click to expand)

  • A student (in the purple sweater) comes over to see what this group is all excited about. The group (especially Alexis, Jorge. and Jose) then explains to her how the wheels move. There is a rubber band connecting the axle with one of the large wheels on the car. When the axle turns, it causes the rubber band to turn, which causes the big wheel to turn, which causes the other big wheel to turn. Near the end of the clip. Sharon rejoins the group and they show her what they have learned.

     

Sharon remains with the group for a while and they share with her about the hot and cold batteries. She is very curious and asks them questions, then asks them to make a prediction.


Video Clip 6 (Day 9)

Some things to notice: (click to expand)
 
Some things to notice: (click to hide)

  • The group is explaining to Sharon that one battery is always hot and one is always cold, and when they switched the batteries the hot one was always in the same position (meaning it was the placement of the batteries, not the batteries themselves, that made one hot).

  • After Sharon asks what would happen if one battery were removed. Assuming she meant the hot battery would be removed, the students predict the wheels would stop turning, but the light would stay lit because the cold battery operates the bulb, whereas the hot battery is connected to the wheels. When Sharon removed the cold battery, the wheels stopped and the light also went off. She asks why, and Jorge says that maybe you need two batteries for anything to work.

     

While Sharon is with the group, they hear a loud commotion coming from the other end of the room. Everyone turns to look and they see several students excited about a discovery they had made with the solar car.
