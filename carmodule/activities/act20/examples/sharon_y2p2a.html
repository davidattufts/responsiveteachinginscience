Summary for Days 5, 6, & 7

After students finished sharing their ideas, they spent over two days talking about and racing the air car, the windup car, and the pullback car.
 

Day 5

At the beginning of the period, the students finished their sharing of ideas that they grouped together. There was some discussion about the air car (propeller car), so as a next move Sharon wanted to discuss that further. Sharon demonstrated the car moving and the kids suggested a race between two air cars.


Video Clip 1 (Day 5)

  • When Isaac and Teresa let the air cars go, his car goes forward and hers goes backwards. Sharon asks the kids what they want to do about it. They respond that they want to have the race again, but this time make sure the propellers are turned the same way. There does not seem to be an agreement on which way the propellers need to be turned so the cars go forward. However, they both do turn the propellers the same way (clockwise looking from the front), and when they are released they both move forward, although one goes straight and the other veers off to the side.

     

Day 6

At the beginning of the period, Sharon asks students if they want to race some of the cars, and of course they enthusiastically agree. However, she tells them that when a particular car wins, she wants them to think about why that happened. Jose then repeats the task.


Video Clip 1 (Day 6)

The students then rearrange themselves to leave open a large space for the race to be conducted. There are three cars in the race: red pullback car, air (propeller) car and key (windup) car. After the race, the students discuss what happened; most students think the pullback car won the race, the air car was second and the windup car was third, this despite the fact that the windup car travelled further than the air car. They then begin to try to explain the results in terms of the weight of the cars.

  • In trying to explain the motion of the cars during the race, Jennifer said that the weight of the car matters: the less weight, the faster the car. Jose disagrees. He said the key car has more weight than the air car, but went further than the air car. When Sharon asks how they know the key car is heavier, a few students say it's heavier because it has metal.

     

Scarlett talks about why the windup car did not go very fast. After other students suggest a name for the coiled metal part of the car, the ‘spiral roller,’ she then compares it to Issac’s previous statement about how a paper tube (telescope) unravels.

Eleanni then tells the class that she thinks the red car won the race, the air car was second, and the windup car was third. Sharon asks the class to vote on whether they agree with Eleanni or not.


Video Clip 4 (Day 6)

After some further discussion, Kayleigh talks about why windup car was very slow, then Alexis talks about why the air car did not go very far.


Video Clip 5 (Day 6)

To make sure the carpet wasn't influencing the race, Issac had suggested they perform the race on the tile floor at the other end of the room. The students gathered and conducted the race again. Teresa handled the red pullback car, Brittany the key car, and Jorge the air car. Afterwards, the students moved back to the carpet to discuss the results, but there was no uniform agreement on which car won the race.


Video Clip 6 (Day 6)

When the class ends, Sharon suggests they could do the race one more time the next period. On Day 7, they conduct the race out in the hallway to give students more room to spread out so a car wouldn't hit any of the students' legs. The pullback car again is the winner, but the other two cars get tangled together and the results are somewhat ambiguous.
