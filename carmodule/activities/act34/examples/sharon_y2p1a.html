Day 11

Gustavo talks about oil on skateboard wheels causing it to go faster because wheels are ‘slippier’. Priscila suggests putting different things, like jelly, on paper, and then the car will go faster. Not everyone agrees. Students suggest other things to put on paper. They think that the surface makes difference (e.g., linoleum and carpet) with respect to the speed of car. Shoes also make a difference in how you move. At end of the period, the class decides on what materials Sharon will bring to class for them to experiment with the next time.
 

Prior to the video clip below, Sharon reminds everyone that they were talking a lot about oil during the previous lesson. She asks students to recount what happened in that conversation. As the students discuss that they think oil will make a car go faster, Priscila proposes that they test whether a car will go faster with different surface materials. This spurs a conversation about what types of surfaces and substances (jelly, jam, peanut butter, oil, whiteboards, wax paper, etc.) will make a car go faster.
