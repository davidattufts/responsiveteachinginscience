Summary for Days 7 & 8

Because the mechanisms of the air car and windup car are transparent, but the mechanism of the pullback car is not, Sharon asks students to imagine what might be inside the pullback car that makes it work. Students draw pictures and explain their ideas.
 

Day 7

At the beginning of the period, Sharon draws her idea for what is inside the pullback car. She ‘thinks’ there is a mouse inside that runs around a big wheel, and that makes the other wheels in the car turn, causing the car to move. She asks the students to spend some time drawing their own pictures and writing their explanations for how the car moves.

  • Priscila refers to how a bicycle works to help explain how the key (windup) car works. In a bicycle, your pedalling pushes the chains, causing the chains to move. The chains are then connected to the tires, causing them to move. A similar thing happens with the key car, but instead of chains there are small pieces (of metal) that are connected to each other. When one moves, it causes the next one to move, and so on, until the wheels are made to move. Her explanations for how the bicycle and key car work are mechanistic. However, in explaining the windup car, she refers to a computer that 'somehow' sends messages to the Dady wheel, which then causes the chain-like wires to move, causing the wheels to turn. The latter part of her explanation for the windup car is mechanistic, but not the first part.

     

Day 8

During the next day, students share with the class their ideas about how the pullback car works. Pricila shares her idea and students ask a lot of questions, especially about the computer that sends messages to the Dady wheel. (See diagram of Pricila's idea above Video Clip 2, Day 7.) Afterwards, Sharon calls on Jamir to share his drawing and ideas. Jamir thinks there is a rubber band inside the car. In the following clip he describes his idea and tries to explain how it becomes thinner as it stretches. He uses an actual rubber band to help illustrate his idea. After describing it, Alexis asks whether the rubber band will break when stretched.


Video Clip 1 (Day 8)

  • Jamir's model is that there is a rubber band inside the car. One end of the rubber band is fixed, and when the car is pulled back, the other end of the rubber band is pulled back, stretching it. (Then, as it unstretches. it makes the car move forward.)

  • Alexis expresses concern that the rubber band will break when stretched too much, but Jamir says that although that may happen for some rubber bands, it won't happen to all rubber bands.

     

Sharon tells Jamir that some students have questions. Jamir calls on Alexis, who wonders whether the rubber band will stay stretched after you let go of the car. Jamir says that no, it will return to its regular size. This is similar to Issac's idea that he had mentioned to Sharon on Day 1, and also to the class on Day 5.
