Summary for Days 3, 4, & 5

After students shared their ideas about the various cars, Sharon asked them to organize some of the class's ideas into groups of ideas that go together. They wrote their groupings on oval cutouts and taped them to the front board. Then students shared the reasons for their particular grouping choices with the whole class.
 

Day 3

In the first video clip Sharon introduces the grouping of ideas task to the students. She wants students to write down some ideas that are similar, draw a circle around them and be able to explain why they are similar. After giving the instructions she calls on some students to repeat the task so that the whole class can have a common understanding of what needs to be done.


Video Clip 1 (Day 3)

The students then break up into groups to works on their grouping of ideas task, and Sharon circulates around the room to engage students in conversation. The next clip shows part of the interaction with the group of Destiny, Scarlett and Pricila, who have grouped together the rubber band, pullback car and slingshot.

Day 4

At the beginning of the next day, Sharon asks students to write the ideas they have grouped together on a cutout oval. They will then tape these to the front of the room and students will come up and share their ideas with the rest of the class. Because her voice is strained on this day she tells student that they will need to be in charge of the conversation.


Video Clip 1 (Day 4)

The next two clips show Pricila sharing her ideas with the class. She had grouped together the rubber band, slingshot and pull back car. The kids ask questions and participate in the conversation, offering suggestions. Sharon plays a limited role, occasionally asking clarifying questions. In the first clip, Jennifer asks whether the pullback car should be in the same group with the rubber band and slingshot. Other students argue why it should be in the same group because they are thinking that a rubber band is inside the pullback car.


Video Clip 2 (Day 4)

  • Priscila claims that the rubber band, slingshot, and pullback car are grouped together because they work the same way: in each case, you pull back a rubber band and let go (she assumes there is a rubber band inside the pullback car).

  • Jennifer challenges the claim that the pullback car should be in the same grouping as the rubber band and slingshot, because in the first case you are pulling the car, and in the other cases you are pulling the rubber band. Jourdan supports Priscila, saying that there is something inside the pullback car that makes it work that is like a rubber band. Jose also supports this, saying there is actually a rubber band inside the pullback car. Priscila says and gestures that pulling the car back with the slingshot and letting go is similar to pulling back the pullback car and letting go--in both cases, the car moves forward.

     

In the next clip, Pricila continues to argue why the rubber band and pullback car should be grouped together. Jamir suggests that the air car should also be included in the same group, but Scarlett argues against it. The discussion centers around whether twisting and pulling (the rubber band) can be considered similar. Several students (Jennifer, Alexis and others) contribute and seem to guide the conversation, without much input from Sharon.

  • Priscila explains how you have to hold the car and rubber band to make the slingshot work (pushing the car forward). Scarlett (identified as 'girl' in the video clip) clarifies the necessity of letting go of both the car and rubber band at the same time for the slingshot to work.

  • Jamir says pulling back the rubber band and car in the slingshot is like twisting the propeller in the air car (propeller car). He thinks the air car should also be grouped in the same circle. Jennifer disagrees, saying that with the air car you are twisting (the rubber band), not pulling it (like the slingshot). Priscila claims that it IS the same, because you are still using a rubber band that needs to be stretched in both cases, except in one case you stretch it by twisting it and in the other case you stretch it by pulling it.

  • Jennifer again brings up a reason why the rubber band should not be in the same grouping as the slingshot: that in one case you are pulling the rubber band and in the other case you are pulling the car. She had mentioned this concern earlier (see previous clip). Priscila disagrees, saying that in both cases you have to pull the thing back; otherwise the car would not go anywhere.

  • In both of the previous two clips, the students are carrying on most of the discussion with little input from Sharon (who had claimed her voice was not strong). As she had asked them to do when she set the task (see Video Clip 1), students were agreeing and disagreeing with Priscila's claim about what her group thought should be put together in the circle.
